  1. Game Rules
    1. You are only permitted one account.
    2. Acceptable language must be used at all times.
    3. Spamming the forums, or other players in the game is not permitted.
    4. You can ONLY log into your own account. If someone goes away on vacation, you are NOT allowed to manage their account. If we find out you are managing someone elses account, both accounts will be deleted.
    5. Deleting and recreating for whatever reason you have, is not allowed. At the time of signing up, double check to be surethat all of your information is correct.
    6. Absolutely no disrespectful comments to the administrators or moderators. If you do not like any of the administrators or moderators for any reason, then do not play the game.

  2. Main Menu
    The Main Menu page is the central source of information on your empire. It tells almost everything about your empire. Gold Pieces, Iron, Lumber, Civilians, Food, Emprie Defence, etc. The Main Menu page also shows your Empire News and links you to Settlement Management (if you are Settlement Leader) and Guild Management (if you are Guild Leader).

  5. Status
    The Status page shows your empire in the greatest detail. It shows how much of anything you make a tick and how many ticks until your troops return from an attack.

  6. Settlement Browser
    From here, you can view every Settlement in the game.

  7. Exploring
    Exploring is one of the 3 ways to gain land or mountains (other 2 ways are attacking and the barter). If you are new to the game, the exporing system can be really complicated so I will do my best to explain it. The more land or mountains you have, the more explorers you need to get 1 land/mountain a tick. To find out how many explorers you need to get 1 land/mountain a tick, just take your land/mountains and divide it by 20. This will give you the number of explorers it takes to get 1 land/mountain a tick. But we have made it easy on you by having the game do this equation for you and showing how many you need right on the explore page.

  8. Mountains
    1. Iron Mines
      Iron Mines mine your Iron. You need Iron to be able to build stronger and more powerful weapons. You can inrease the output of each mine by researching Advanced Iron Mining.
    2. Gold Mines
      Gold mines mine your Gold Pieces. This is the currency in Medieval Battles. As with iron mining you can increase the output of each mine by researching Advanced Gold Mining.

  9. Land
    1. Homes
      Homes are the most important building in Medieval Battles. Each home provides enough space for 20 Civillians without Homes you have no population which means no Recruits and no new troops, Thieves or Scientists.
    2. Barracks
      Barracks house your military units: warriors, priests, wizards and archers. Each barrack holds 50 troops (unless your race is Human then this is reduced to 20). Always remember to have enough room in your barracks for more men. If you have no room you will not be able to build more any more troops until you do have room.
    3. Farms
      Farms are needed to produce food for your population. Without them your population will die. A good way to keep your population always food is to build 1 Farm for every 2 Home. Much less than this and you will not produce enough food.
    4. Wooden Platforms
      These are wooden defence towers. Each one will add 10 points to your Empire Defence. So the more you have, the more your defence will be. A good tip on these is to build lots at a time. If you only have 10 or so Wooden Platforms, they will not do much for your empire and you would be better off using that land for something else. But if you are going to build them, make sure you have enough. Enough so that they make a difference in your empire's defence.
    5. Lumber Mills
      Lumber Mills produce Lumber for you. After researching Archery, you will be able to build bows. You must research Archery before you can build Lumber Mills (unless your class is Ranger then this is already complete). Each Lumber Mill produces 2 lumber per tick.

  10. Military
    1. Sages
      Sages are used to research items. One Sage equals 1 research point each tick, unless your race or class says otherwise.
    2. Thieves
      Thieves are used to gain intelligence on other empires. The more you have, the better your chances of getting information on their empire. Also, the more you have, the harder it is for others to gain intelligence on your empire.
    3. Explorers
      Explorers are used to explore for land or mountains. To understand our exploring system, go       here.
    4. Warriors
      Warriors are to be used for attacking or defending.
    5. Priests
      Priests are to be used for attacking or defending.
    6. Wizards
      Wizards are to be used for attacking or defending.
    7. Archers
      Archers are to be used for attacking or defending. Archers are gained when you research Archery.
    8. Catapults
      Catapults are to be used for defending. Catapults are gained when you research Archery.
    9. Suicide Civilians
      Suicide Civilians are to be used for attacking or defending. They are gained when you research Demolitions.
    10. Golem
      Golems are gained when you research Animation. 2 Wizards can "animate" into a Golem for attacking or defending.
    11. Iron Golem
      Iron Golems are gained when you research AdvancedAnimation. Like the Golem, Iron Golems are made when 2 Wizards can "animate" into an Iron Golem for attacking or defending.

  11. Recruits
    Recruits come when you get more Civilians. The more Civilians you have, the more Recruits you get a tick. To see how many Recruits you make a tick, check on the Status page.

  25. Barter
    In the barter, you are able to put up any number (as long as it is not more than half of your holdings) of units, resources, land or mountains. Other empires can then bid on the item for 4 ticks. The person who has the highest bid at the end of the 4 ticks wins the item. Think Ebay©, only with a fantasy feel.

  26. Trade
    Trading is a new feature here at Medieval Battles. When you go to the Trade page, you are given the option of creating a trade route, or sending resources/units to an empire you have a trade route with. All trade routes last for 40 ticks due to "wear and tear". And after you send aid to an empire, you have to wait 6 ticks before you can send aid again. This is put in place to discourage cheating.

  27. Attack
    1. Resource Attack
      When you do a Resource Attack, you are trying to get: Gold Pieces, Iron, Lumber and Civilians. If successful, you will get 10% of their resources.
    2. Mountain Attack
      The main objective of a Mountain Attack is to gain Mountains. If successful you will receive 10% of their Mountains. So if they have 100 Mountains, you will get 10. If they have 1000, you will get 100.
    3. Land Attack
      When you do a Land Attack, you are trying to get Land. If successful you will get 10% of their Land. So if they have 100 land, you will get 10. If they have 1000, you will get 100.
    4. Suicide Attack
      When you execute a Suicide Attack, you send Suicide Civilians into someones empire to kill Civilians and Thieves. 1 Suicide Civilian kills 1 Civilian or Thieve. Whatever you send, you lose forever. Hence the name, Suicide Attack. In order to do a Suicide Attack, you must first research Pyrotechnics to get the Suicide Civilian unit.

  28. Intelligence
    The Intelligence page is where you use your Thieves to get information on an empire. The more Theives you have, the better the chance of you gaining the Intelligence.
